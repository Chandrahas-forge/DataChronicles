Properties of Eigenvectors

Eigenvectors have unique properties:

  • Direction Preservation: They preserve their line of action, scaling by the eigenvalue. Positive eigenvalues preserve direction, while negative eigenvalues flip direction.
  • Linearity: Eigenvectors of a matrix form a linear space.
  • Invariant Subspace: The span of the eigenvectors is an invariant subspace underAAA.

Geometric Interpretation

Eigenvectors represent directions in which a linear transformation acts by stretching or compressing. For our example:

  • v1v_1v1​represents the direction along whichAAAscales by 3.
  • v2v_2v2​represents the direction along whichAAAscales by 1.

Connecting Eigenvectors to Previous Concepts

Previously, we examined how matricesAAAtransform vectors through various operations, altering their directions and magnitudes. By imposingAx=λxAx = \lambda xAx=λx, we introduce a special condition where certain vectors, called eigenvectors, retain their direction (or flip if the eigenvalue (λ\lambdaλ) is negative), scaled by(λ\lambdaλ). This reveals invariant subspaces where eigenvectors remain in their span, showcasing directions that the transformation (AAA) leaves unchanged. Thus, the eigenvalue equation identifies special vectors that provide simplified and insightful views of the transformation, maintaining proportional scaling rather than altering directions.
